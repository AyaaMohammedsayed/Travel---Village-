Pyramids

The Giza pyramid complex consists of the Great Pyramid (also known as the Pyramid of Cheops or Cheops and constructed around 2580-2560 BC), the somewhat smaller Pyramid of Khafre located a few hundred meters to the southwest, and the relatively modest Pyramid of Menkaure located a few miles away. Hundreds of meters to the southwest. As for the Sphinx, it is located on the eastern side of the complex. Most Egyptologists today agree that the head of the Sphinx is the head of King Khafre. In addition to these main monuments, there are a number of smaller monuments known as the “Queens” Pyramids, bridges, and valley pyramids.

DAHAB

The former Bedouin fishing village of Dahab is now a popular tourist destination, especially for adventurous surfers, who will find some of the best conditions in the world at its beaches. This city has long been known as a quiet and camping town and has always attracted travelers and backpackers. It is now more developed, but retains its ordinary and traditional character. After all, it is also the location of the Blue Hole, one of the most dangerous diving spots in the world.
